INFLUENZA

Influenza (flu) is a highly contagious disease caused by influenza viruses that infect the nose, throat, and sometimes the lungs. It can cause mild to severe illness, and at times can lead to death. It spreads through body fluids from infected people. It is usually prevented by vaccination every year and treated by managing symptoms.

Influenza in the World

Influenza activity has decreased or remained low in most countries now, except in some countries in tropical countries in Africa, Americas, and Asia, where influenza acitivity increased. World Health Organization (WHO) has Global Influenza Programme. In this program, there is a global web-based tool for influenza virological surveillance which is called FluNet. It will collect data (the number of influenza viruses detected by subtype) from National Influenza Centres (NICs) and other national influenza laboratories from 99 countries, areas, or territories. The data at country level are publically available and updated weekly in their webpage.

Influenza in Australia

Influenza is a seasonal infection that usually occurs from April to September. Flu seasons vary in severity and duration each year. In a year of high Influenza activity, it can caused more than 3,300 deaths in Australia. Australia have 2 department called National Influenza Surveillance Scheme (NISS) and National Notifiable Diseases Surveillance System (NNDSS) that annually reports Influenza each year.
